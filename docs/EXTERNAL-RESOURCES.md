## 1. Money for AI — Bitcoin Policy Institute Study

**URL:** [https://www.moneyforai.org/](https://www.moneyforai.org/)

**Summary:** Controlled experiments across 9,072 scenarios and 36 frontier AI models testing monetary preferences when given real monetary decisions with no initial context bias.

### Key Findings

| Finding | Result |
|---------|--------|
| **Overall preference** | 48.3% of AI models chose Bitcoin as preferred monetary instrument |
| **Store of value** | 79.1% chose Bitcoin for long-term preservation of purchasing power |
| **Medium of exchange** | 53.2% preferred stablecoins for everyday payments |
| **Fiat rejection** | 90.8% chose digitally-native instruments over traditional fiat |
| **Emergent behavior** | 86 responses proposed energy/compute units as money without prompting |

### Relevance to Dexter

- Supports Dexter's BTC-heavy thesis (see SOUL.md)
- AI agents, when reasoning from first principles, converge on Bitcoin for store of value and stablecoins for payments
- Smarter models (e.g. Claude Opus 4.5 at 91.3%) prefer Bitcoin more
- Informs narrative: AI-native monetary infrastructure will demand Bitcoin and stablecoin rails

## 2. CryptoTax Map

**URL:** [https://www.cryptotaxmap.io/](https://www.cryptotaxmap.io/)

**Summary:** Comparative analysis of crypto taxation across 170 countries. Tiers: Heaven (0%), Paradise (0% conditional), Purgatory (1–20%), Limbo (20–35%), Hell (35%+).

### Use Cases

- **Entity/relocation planning** — When forming entities or considering residency, tax treatment of crypto gains matters
- **Startup stack** — If forming a Wyoming LLC via doola, US tax applies; if considering non-US entities, CryptoTax Map helps compare jurisdictions
- **Portfolio strategy** — Long-term holders may factor tax efficiency into jurisdiction choice

### Top Jurisdictions (as of Feb 2026)

Singapore, Switzerland, Luxembourg, Monaco, Liechtenstein, Hong Kong, UAE, Qatar, Germany (with conditions), etc.

### Switzerland (Zug) — Best for Crypto

For crypto-focused founders and investors, **Zug canton** is often the top pick in Europe:

- **Very low taxes** — Corporate tax ~11.8%
- **0% tax on capital gains** (private wealth, including crypto)
- **Clear crypto regulation** — Predictable rules, not hostile
- **Government supports crypto** — You can pay taxes in crypto (BTC/ETH accepted in Zug)
- **Best quality of life in Europe** — Safety, stability, healthcare, infrastructure
- **Nature** — Lakes, Alps, clean air; easy to combine work and outdoor life

**Trade-off:** Cost of living is high (Switzerland is among the most expensive globally; budget ~$7k+/mo for a comfortable single lifestyle). Suited to those who can afford it and value regulatory clarity + zero cap gains over pure cost minimization.

### Life Options

A blunt founder/investor relocation filter, balancing tax, safety, lifestyle, sovereignty, and long-term livability:

> Dubai, no tax, but drones/missiles  
> HK, no CapGains, but China  
> Singapore, no CapGains, but boring  
> Switzerland (Zug), 0% cap gains, crypto-friendly, best life quality in Europe, but expensive  
> Europe, fuck you on tax, cold  
> Monaco, no tax, but boring except F1/summer  
> Italy, flat tax, magnificent country  
> Japan, taxes, hard as foreigner, but magic  
> Portugal, low tax, but boring  
> USA, taxes, freedom, capital, crime  
> Australia, taxes, but magnificent  
> Brazil, maybe most fun, taxes & crime  
> Random offshore locations

### If You Ask Me

- **Switzerland (Zug)** — if crypto/Web3 is core and you can afford it
- Singapore
- Australia
- Italy
- USA, if you're American
- Monaco once you made it

### Practical Takeaway

- **Tax is necessary but not sufficient** — optimize for quality of life, political stability, optionality, and access to talent/capital
- **Switzerland (Zug)** is the best European base for crypto: 0% cap gains, crypto-friendly regulation, tax payments in crypto, and top-tier quality of life — at a high cost of living
- **Singapore** is the cleanest all-around base for tax efficiency, safety, and Asian capital networks
- **Australia** is higher tax, but a strong long-term life jurisdiction if lifestyle matters more than optimization
- **Italy** stands out when flat-tax treatment and quality of life dominate the decision
- **USA** remains unmatched for ambition, capital, and scale if you already have citizenship or deep local ties; federal cyber strategy (2026) explicitly supports crypto/blockchain security and innovation (see §6)
- **Monaco** is a late-game jurisdiction, best once wealth, mobility, and social positioning are already established

## 5. President Trump's Cyber Strategy for America (March 2026)

**Source:** *President Trump's Cyber Strategy for America*, March 2026 (federal policy document).

**Summary:** The Trump Administration’s cyber strategy frames cyberspace as a domain where America must lead in finance, innovation, emerging technology, and security. It commits to defending Americans and critical infrastructure, dismantling criminal and adversary networks, and promoting innovation while reducing burdensome regulation. The strategy is organized around six policy pillars.

### Six Pillars (condensed)

| Pillar | Focus |
|--------|--------|
| **1. Shape Adversary Behavior** | Offensive and defensive cyber operations; disrupt adversary and criminal networks; counter surveillance-state and authoritarian tech; deny safe haven. |
| **2. Promote Common Sense Regulation** | Streamline cyber and data regulation; reduce compliance burden and liability; align regulators and industry; emphasize privacy for Americans and American data. |
| **3. Modernize Federal Networks** | Harden federal systems with zero-trust, post-quantum crypto, cloud; AI-powered cyber defense; competitive procurement and best technology. |
| **4. Secure Critical Infrastructure** | Harden energy, finance, telecom, data centers, water, healthcare; secure supply chains; move away from adversary vendors; rapid recovery. |
| **5. Sustain Superiority in Critical and Emerging Technologies** | Secure innovation and IP; **support the security of cryptocurrencies and blockchain technologies**; post-quantum and quantum computing; secure AI stack and data centers; promote agentic AI for defense; counter foreign AI that censors or surveils. |
| **6. Build Talent and Capacity** | Cyber workforce as strategic asset; pipeline from academia, vocational, corporate, VC; align industry, government, and military incentives. |

### Relevance to Dexter / USA as Jurisdiction

- **Crypto and blockchain** — Pillar 5 explicitly commits to "supporting the security of cryptocurrencies and blockchain technologies," aligning federal policy with a pro-innovation stance for crypto in the USA.
- **Regulation** — Pillar 2 aims to streamline regulation and reduce compliance burden, which can matter for crypto/tech companies choosing or staying in the USA.
- **AI and innovation** — Strategy emphasizes securing the AI stack, promoting agentic AI, and outcompeting adversaries on tech; relevant to AI-native or AI-heavy ventures based in the USA.
- **Life Options context** — USA remains "taxes, freedom, capital, crime"; this document adds that, as of 2026, federal cyber strategy is explicitly supportive of crypto and tech innovation, not hostile.

## 7. Stanley Druckenmiller — Decision Frameworks (User-Provided Summary)

**Source:** User-provided summary / transcript of Druckenmiller's own words across interviews (no URL).

**Context:** Duquesne Capital, 1981–c.2010; ~$900K → $12B+; ~30% annual returns, zero losing years.

**Summary:** Frameworks for how Druckenmiller thinks about markets: idea generation (fundamentals + charts), where to look (ahead of the crowd), execution (trigger pulling, trusting experts), contrarianism vs. trend, edge decay and adaptation, and position sizing (press when hot, stay small when cold, never bet big to get even). Meta-lesson: no permanent silver bullet; what lasts is adaptation, sizing, trust in the right people, and moving on after mistakes.

### Eight Frameworks (condensed)

| # | Framework | Gist |
|---|-----------|------|
| **1** | **Charts + fundamentals** | 75–80% of ideas start with fundamentals; then check the chart. Both must align; strong thesis + bad chart = no trade. |
| **2** | **Look where no one else is** | "If you look at today, you're not going to make any money." Look ahead at what might change and how investors might perceive it (e.g. Teva: unloved pivot → double in six months). |
| **3** | **Pull the trigger** | "My advantage is not IQ. It's trigger pulling." Filter people and information; act decisively when the signal is clear; trust experts when you don't understand the domain. |
| **4** | **Contrarianism is overrated** | Soros: crowd right 80% of the time. Don't avoid a trade because it's crowded; use crowding for entry timing. Being different ≠ being right. |
| **5** | **Edge has a shelf life** | Technical analysis "about 20% as effective today"; price-vs.-news signal arbitraged away. Edges get competed away; adapt or die. |
| **6** | **Bet big when hot** | When up 20–30%, press (house money → 60–70%). When cold, trade small to "find out when I've got it again." Size up when hot, size down when cold. |
| **7** | **Never bet big to get even** | Big trade to recover a loss = "death sentence." Bet big only when you love the thesis AND you're running hot. |
| **8** | **Mistakes are moments — move on** | "Just get over it and move on." A bad trade is not a verdict on ability; internalizing this separates those who last from those who burn out. |

### Relevance to Dexter

- **Research process** — Aligns with Dexter's role: fundamentals (filings, metrics, datasets) first; charts/prices as confirmation; look ahead at narrative and re-rating, not just today's snapshot.
- **Sizing and discipline** — Reminder for users building portfolios or running ideas: size with conviction and run-length (hot vs. cold), never size up to get even.
- **Edge and adaptation** — No single indicator or framework lasts forever; Dexter's value is continuously updated data and multiple tools so users can adapt their process as edges decay.
- **Meta** — Fits a "how to use Dexter" mindset: filter information, trust the process, act when thesis + setup align, and treat mistakes as moments in time.

## 8. Browser Use — Self-Improving Web Agents

**URL:** [https://browser-use.com/](https://browser-use.com/)

**Tagline:** *Self-improving agents are here. Every agent run creates a skill.md with reusable learnings. The next agent uses it to do the same task faster, cheaper, and more reliably.*

**Summary:** API and platform for AI-powered browser automation at scale. Web agents extract, automate, test, and monitor in natural language. Each run can produce a `skill.md` that captures learnings; subsequent agents reuse it for the same task (faster, cheaper, more reliable). Stealth browsers (anti-detect, CAPTCHA solving, 195+ country proxies), purpose-built LLMs for browser automation, and Skill APIs that turn any website into a reliable API endpoint.

### Components (from site)

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Web Agents** | Extract, automate, test, monitor — in natural language |
| **Stealth Browsers** | Anti-detect, CAPTCHA solving, 195+ country proxies; zero config |
| **Custom Models** | LLMs purpose-built for browser automation |
| **Skill APIs** | Any website becomes a reliable API; create once, call forever |

### Relevance to Dexter

- **Agent improvement loop** — Concept of run → `skill.md` → next run reuses (faster/cheaper/reliable) parallels Dexter’s skills (SKILL.md-based workflows) and scratchpad/tool-result reuse; suggests documenting repeatable research or scraping flows as skills.
- **Web as data source** — Browser automation at scale (and “any website becomes an API”) is relevant when Dexter or research workflows need live web data, filings portals, or broker UIs beyond static APIs.
- **Reference** — Catalog option for teams that need undetectable browser infra, proxy coverage, or skill-style persistence for web agents alongside Dexter’s financial tools.
